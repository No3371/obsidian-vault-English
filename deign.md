# deign

*verb*

 disapproving


us /deɪn/ 
uk /deɪn/

 **deign to do sth**

to do something [unwillingly](https://dictionary.cambridge.org/us/dictionary/english/unwillingly "unwillingly") and in a way that [shows](https://dictionary.cambridge.org/us/dictionary/english/show "shows") that you [think](https://dictionary.cambridge.org/us/dictionary/english/think "think") you are too [important](https://dictionary.cambridge.org/us/dictionary/english/important "important") to do it:

>If she deigns to [reply](https://dictionary.cambridge.org/us/dictionary/english/reply "reply") to my [letter](https://dictionary.cambridge.org/us/dictionary/english/letter "letter"), I'll be [extremely](https://dictionary.cambridge.org/us/dictionary/english/extremely "extremely") [surprised](https://dictionary.cambridge.org/us/dictionary/english/surprised "surprised").

**Synonym**

[condescend to do sth](https://dictionary.cambridge.org/us/dictionary/english/condescend "meaning of condescend to do sth") usually humorous
